Website & Technical Foundation

Why AI Doesn't Trust Your Site for Specialty Queries

  • 5 team pages show placeholder identity names. Queen Ledner, Raphaelle Kemmer, Ryleigh Altenwerth, Stuart Schuster, and Trent Romaguera pattern-match output from a common developer testing library. Only Dr. Wolfson and Dr. Nolan appear as real staff on-site. AI systems flag auto-generated identity content as an E-E-A-T negative.
  • Homepage H1 is "Dentistry designed around you". Zero service keyword. Zero geo keyword. The most authoritative page on your domain does not tell Google or AI that you are a dentist in Baltimore. 15-minute fix, immediate lift on "Baltimore dentist" queries.
  • Service pages use the wrong schema type. Dental services are marked up with Product schema (meant for physical goods). AI knowledge graphs expect Dentist, MedicalBusiness, or MedicalProcedure. Swap on all 10 service pages.
  • Four service pages have FAQ content but no FAQPage schema. Implants (5 Qs), veneers (4 Qs), Invisalign (8 Qs), emergency (4 Qs). The content is there, but it is not machine-readable for AI Overviews or ChatGPT citation.
  • Emergency dentistry page promises same-day service. Office hours are Mon-Thu only. No on-call number listed for Friday through Sunday. AI systems cross-check hours with service claims and discount the one that contradicts the other.
