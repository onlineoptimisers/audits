Top 3 Critical Findings

Finding 01

Five team pages carry non-real identity names

The sitemap includes profile pages under Queen Ledner, Raphaelle Kemmer, Ryleigh Altenwerth, Stuart Schuster, and Trent Romaguera. The only dentists named on the homepage and about page are Dr. Erin Wolfson and Dr. Sydney Nolan. These names match the pattern of Faker.js / template output. Either populate with real staff (hygienists, dental assistants) with Person schema and photos, or noindex and drop from the sitemap. This is an E-E-A-T risk and a trust signal that AI retrievers and reviewers will flag.

Finding 02

FAQPage schema absent on every service page

Every checked service page (implants, veneers, Invisalign, emergencies) has FAQ content in plain text, 4 to 8 questions each. None are wrapped in FAQPage JSON-LD. One ~2 hour dev ticket unlocks FAQ rich-result eligibility and increased likelihood of citation on conversational and problem-based queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

Finding 03

Strong SEO foundation masks an entity-signal gap

Domain Rank 148, 238 ranked keywords, 28 in top 10, $1419/mo ETV, 900+ reviews at 4.9 stars. The gap is not content or authority, it is entity signal clarity. Product schema is used for dental services (wrong @type). Person schema is missing for both dentists. These fixes are the lever from Fair to Good on the AI Visibility score.

Where the opportunity sits

Hamilton ranks for 238 keywords in the top 50, sits at DR 148, and already wins the #1 Maps position for "family dentist Baltimore MD" and the Hamilton-Lauraville hyperlocal query. AI platforms cite Hamilton on 28 of 60 query-platform slots. The remaining 32 invisible slots are overwhelmingly high-value specialty queries: implants, veneers, Invisalign, full-mouth reconstruction. These are $3K-30K cases per patient. The gap closing between where you rank today and where you rank after the fixes in this audit represents measurable new revenue. 11Expert Strategic Insights▼

Seven cross-referenced findings connecting data points.
Insight 01

Five team pages read as non-real identities

Queen Ledner, Raphaelle Kemmer, Ryleigh Altenwerth, Stuart Schuster, and Trent Romaguera have profile pages in the sitemap. The only dentists named on the homepage and about page are Dr. Erin Wolfson and Dr. Sydney Nolan. This pattern matches Faker.js template output. Populate with real staff and Person schema, or noindex and remove. E-E-A-T integrity for a medical practice is non-negotiable.

Insight 02

FAQPage schema is the single biggest quick win

Every service page already has FAQ content in plain text. None are wrapped in FAQPage JSON-LD. One ~2 hour dev ticket unlocks rich-result eligibility and increased likelihood of citation across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews on question-style queries.

Insight 03

Product schema is the wrong @type for a medical practice

On-page content uses Product schema for dental services. The correct entity types are MedicalBusiness for the clinic, MedicalProcedure for each treatment, and Service where generic. Keep the AggregateRating. Swap on the 10 service pages.

Insight 04

Striking distance is loaded on emergency keywords

Three of the top 15 striking distance keywords are emergency-related at positions 11-17 (emergency dentist Baltimore city, county, maryland - each 1,600 monthly searches). The landing page already ranks. Fix the Friday-Sunday hours tension and add schema, and page-one is within reach for 4,800+ monthly searches.

Insight 05

Neighborhood dominance is a defensible moat

Hamilton ranks #1 in Maps for "family dentist Baltimore MD" and owns the Hamilton-Lauraville hyperlocal query. No competitor is claiming this frame. Neighborhood cluster pages for Hamilton, Lauraville, Parkville, Overlea, Hamilton Hills deepen the moat. Competitors are fighting a broader Baltimore battle with 20-plus practices bidding. The neighborhood frame has a field of one.

Insight 06

Author entity signal is missing for both dentists

Both Dr. Wolfson and Dr. Nolan have bios on the about page. Neither has Person schema with sameAs links to LinkedIn, ADA profile, Healthgrades, or other professional registries. Person schema with sameAs is the strongest E-E-A-T author signal for medical content.

Insight 07

Specialty invisibility is the revenue story

The site has 2300+ word service pages, 900+ reviews at 4.9 stars, 500+ CME hours, and an advanced GP residency credential. On ChatGPT implants-Baltimore and veneers-Baltimore queries, Hamilton is not cited. The gap is schema plus medical-directory mirror claims. These are $3K-30K cases. Every month of invisibility has a revenue tag.
