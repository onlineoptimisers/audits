Your three pre-call questions, answered straight

What is the average cost per booked call?
For HVAC in Alberta, you should expect $25 to $90 per booked call as the realistic working range. Meta lead ads average $35 to $55 when optimized (Forward First Media 2025). Google Search Ads sit higher: roughly $34 per branded lead, $72 per Performance Max lead, and $149 per non-branded search lead, with national average around $104 (BuiltRight Digital 2026). Local SEO and AI ranking start more expensive on a per-lead basis in months 1 to 3 because of the upfront investment, then drop to $15 to $60 per booked call as rankings and citations compound. The DataForSEO live pull on your top Lethbridge HVAC keywords shows CPCs from $7.68 (duct cleaning) to $31.89 (furnace) per click in 2026, which converts at typical 6 to 10 percent landing-page rates into the CPL ranges shown.
Sources: BuiltRight Digital, Forward First Media, AxZ Lead, DataForSEO Google Ads (Lethbridge AB, live 2026-04-16).
What percent of those typically turn into closed jobs?
For HVAC contractors in 2025: 38 percent close rate without financing offered, 49 percent with financing offered (ACHR News 2025 contractor survey). Industry-wide booking rate (lead to scheduled appointment) sits around 42 percent. Where you sit in that range depends on three things: speed-to-lead (responding within 5 minutes can lift conversion up to 400 percent per Plecto 2025), whether you offer financing, and the channel the lead came from (organic Maps and AI-citation leads close at the high end, cold paid social at the low end). For your Breathe Easy operation with Sandi handling intake and a 4.7-star reputation, hitting the 40 to 50 percent close range is realistic on properly qualified inbound.
Sources: ACHR News 2025 HVAC contractor survey, Plecto HVAC sales metrics 2025, Lokal HQ HVAC KPIs 2025.
What does this usually look like for HVAC in our region (Lethbridge / Southern Alberta)?
Lethbridge is a focused 100k-population market with sharp seasonality. Demand peaks twice: heating season Oct to Mar (furnace repair, install, emergency calls) and AC season Jun to Aug. Search volume backs this up: "furnace repair lethbridge" 90/mo, "hvac lethbridge" 140/mo, "duct cleaning lethbridge" 320/mo (DataForSEO 2026). The local market is dominated by Charlton and Hill (since 1941, runs the AI answers and the top of Google). 4 Seasons Home Comfort and Airtech are the next tier on reviews. You sit fourth on review-driven queries (171 reviews, 4.7 stars, 12 years) but invisible on the higher-intent "best HVAC" queries because your schema, entity, and citation layer is incomplete (your own audit, 2026-04-16). Average Canadian HVAC ticket sizes for budget math: furnace install $4,000 to $8,000, AC install $5,500 average, heat pump $6,000 to $15,000, service call $300 to $450. Alberta sits in the middle of that national range. Winter emergency demand is your biggest unfair advantage, that is when ad CPLs climb 20 to 40 percent for everyone but a Maps + AI-cited result wins the booking.
Sources: DataForSEO Google Ads Search Volume (Lethbridge AB, 2026-04-16), Hawana HVAC Canada 2025 install costs, RenoAssistance 2025 HVAC pricing, Norcan Heating Edmonton 2025 install costs, Schulze Creative 2026 HVAC seasonal CPC data, internal audit 2026-04-16.
